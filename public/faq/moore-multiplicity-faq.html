Moore Multiplicity Model ©

A holistic framework developed by Margaret Moore integrating nine perspectives of mind, motivation, and leadership.

Overview

The Moore Multiplicity Model © integrates decades of personality, motivation, and leadership science into nine distinct yet interconnected dimensions. Each represents a way of perceiving, valuing, and acting in the world.

It’s called “Multiplicity” because each person embodies multiple perspectives — logical, emotional, creative, disciplined, and so on. The model organizes this richness so we can understand and balance the voices within us.

The Nine Dimensions

Everyone has access to all nine dimensions. They are not personality types but perspectives that can be developed to create inner balance and stronger leadership.

1. Autonomy

Authentic / “I care”

Integrity, values, doing what feels right regardless of consequence.

2. Relational

Relational / “I help”

Empathy, compassion, connection, and awareness of others’ needs.

3. Regulator

Servant / “I serve”

Stability, responsibility, discipline, and protection from chaos.

4. Adventurer

Agile / “I flex”

Curiosity, spontaneity, energy, and resilience in uncertainty.

5. Thinker

Conscious / “I see clearly”

Logic, systems thinking, evidence-based analysis, mindfulness.

6. Achiever

Shared / “I share”

Drive, accountability, results orientation, teamwork.

7. Meaning-Maker

Compassion / “I resonate”

Purpose, perspective, wisdom, connection to something larger.

8. Creative

Transformation / “I transform”

Innovation, imagination, synthesis, seeing new possibilities.

9. Confidence

Positive / “I strengthen”

Agency, courage, optimism, belief in one’s own capacity to act.

Leadership Applications

Each dimension also reflects a leadership capacity — how we express influence, purpose, and presence.

  • Autonomy (Authentic) — Leads from integrity and personal conviction.
  • Relational (Relational) — Builds trust through empathy and connection.
  • Regulator (Servant) — Creates stability, order, and safety for others.
  • Adventurer (Agile) — Adapts quickly, leads through experimentation.
  • Thinker (Conscious) — Clarifies complexity, enables collective sense-making.
  • Achiever (Shared) — Motivates through goals, communication, and results.
  • Meaning-Maker (Compassion) — Inspires purpose and reflection.
  • Creative (Transformation) — Catalyzes innovation and change.
  • Confidence (Positive) — Builds strength and optimism in teams.

Scientific Foundations

The model synthesizes validated frameworks from multiple fields:

  • Personality psychology (Big Five, Hartman-Molina axiology)
  • Cognitive science (Jungian functions, Multiple Intelligences)
  • Positive psychology (Character strengths, resilience research)
  • Motivation theory (Enneagram, developmental models)
  • Wisdom traditions (Daoism, I Ching, biocognitive culture)
Key Sources: Organize Your Emotions, Optimize Your Mind (Harvard Health, 2016)
The Science of Leadership: Nine Ways to Upgrade Your Impact (Berrett-Koehler, 2025)

Complete Map

The Moore Multiplicity Model aligns traits, values, cognitive processes, and motivations across nine perspectives, forming a map of whole-person development and integrative leadership.

Each perspective contributes distinct insight — logic, empathy, creativity, discipline, purpose — but wholeness comes from balance. The aim is not perfection in one dimension but integration of all nine.
